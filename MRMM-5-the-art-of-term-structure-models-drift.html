The Art of Term Structure Models: Drift

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Model 1: Normal Distribution/No Drift

dr = σdw

'r' = the rate. 'σ√(dt)' is the standard deviation of the change in the rate. In practice, this model does not accurately reflect term structure rates and has a downward-sloping term structure due to the absence of drift and the effect of convexity.

Model 2: Drift and Risk Premium

dr = λdt + σdw

This model incorporates drift (interpreted as a risk premium) in order to build a more economically robust model.

The Ho-Lee Model: Time-Dependent Drift

dr = λtdt + σdw

In contrast to model 2, the drift is time-dependent.

The Vasicek Model: Mean Reversion

  • dr = k(θ-r)dt + σdw
  • Non-recombining rate tree
    • 'θ' is the long-run value (or central tendency) of the short-term rate and 'k' is a constant denoting the speed of mean reversion.
