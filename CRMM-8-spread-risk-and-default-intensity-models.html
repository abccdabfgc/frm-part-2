Spread Risk and Default Intensity Models

Credit Spreads

Definition: The compensation the market offers for bearing default risk

  • Yield spread
  • i-spread
  • z-spread
  • Asset-swap spread
  • Credit default swap spread
  • Option-adjusted spread (OAS)
  • Discount margin
The yield spread is the difference between the YTM of a credit-risky bond and that of a benchmark gov't bond. The i-spread is the difference between the yield of a credit-risky bond and the linearly interpolated yield between the two bechnmark gov't bonds or swap rates with maturities flanking that of the credit-risk bond. The z-spread is the spread that must be added to the LIBOR spot curve to arrive at the market price of the bond. The asset-swap spread is the quoted margin on the floating leg of an asset swap on a bond. The credit default swap spread is the market premium of a CDS on similar bonds of the same issuer. The OAS is a variant of the z-spread that accounts for embeded options. The discount margin is a spread concept applied to floating-rate notes.

Default Curve Analytics

  • Default risk for a single company can be represented as a Bernoulli trial
  • Hazard rate/default intensity - the parameter driving default
  • Default time distribution function - the probability of default between now and time 't'
  • Default time density function - the derivative of the default time distribution
  • Conditional default probability

Spread Risk

Definition: The risk of loss from changes in the pricing of credit-risk securities

  • Spread volatility - the degree to which spreads fluctuate over time
