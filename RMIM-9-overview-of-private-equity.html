Types of Investments

Leveraged Buyout (LBO)

Refers to the purchase of all or most of a company or business unit using equity from a small group of investors in combination with a significant amount of debt. The targets are typically mature companies that generate strong operating cash flow

Growth Capital

Typically refers to minority equity investments in mature companies that need capital to expand or restructure operations, finance an acquisition, or enter a new market, without a change of control

Mezzanine Capital

Refers to an investment in subordinated debt or preferred stock of a company, without taking voting control. The securities often have warrants or conversion rights attached

Venture Capital

Refers to equity investments in less mature non-public companies to fund the launch, early development, or expansion of a business

Characteristics of an LBO Transaction

  • Company or business unit acquired financed with secured debt and equity
  • Relatively high leverage
  • Results in company going private with a view towards an eventual exit strategy
  • IRR determined by amount of leverage, dividends, and eventual exit
  • General partners commit capital alongside limited partners

Target Company Characteristics

  • Motivated and competent management
  • Robust and stable cash flow
  • Leveragable balance sheet
  • Low capital expenditures
  • Quality assets
  • Opportunities for asset sales and cost-cutting
