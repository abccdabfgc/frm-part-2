Structured Credit Basics

Structured Products

  • Types:
    • Covered bonds
    • Mortgage pass-through securities
    • Collateralized mortgage obligations
    • Structured credit products
  • Often housed in bankruptcy-remote special purpose entities (SPEs)
  • Classification dimensions:
    • Underlying asset classes
    • Type of structure
    • How much pool changes over time: static, revolving, and managed pools

Capital Structure and Credit Losses

  • Capital structure is broken into distinct tranches
    • Equity
    • Junior debt
    • Senior debt
  • The boundary between tranches is referred to as the "attachment point"
  • The portion of the capital structure below a tranche is referred to as its credit enhancement
  • Overcollateralization is the technique of selling a par amount of bonds that is less than the par amount of underlying collateral

Waterfall

Definition: The rules governing how cash flows are distributed to the various securities in the capital structure

Issuance Process

  • Players:
    • Loan originator - original lender who creates the debt obligations in the collateral pool
    • Underwriter - aggregates the underlying loans, designs the securitization structure, and markets the liabilities
    • Rating agencies - assess the creditworthiness of the liabilities and assign a rating
    • Servicers and managers - collects principal and interest and disburses it to the liability holders

Credit Scenario Analysis

  1. Track the interim cash flows
  2. Track the terminal year cash flows
    • Loan interest from surviving loans
    • Proceeds from redemptions at par of the surviving loans
    • Recover from defaulting loans
    • Value of overcollateralization account

Simulating Structured Credit Risk

  • Procedure:
    1. Estimate parameters
    2. Generate default time simulations
    3. Compute credit losses
  • Key characteristics:
    • Increases in the default rate increase bond losses and decrease equity IRR
    • Increases in correlation has varying effects, depending on the level of defaults
    • At low correlations, equity values exhibit positive convexity in default rates and the senior bond tranche exhibits negative convexity in defaul rates

Structured Credit Risks

  • Systematic risk
  • Tranche thinness
  • Granularity - diminishes concentration risk

Default Correlation Concepts

  • Default correlation - correlation concept most directly related to portfolio credit risk
  • Asset return correlation - correlation of logarithmic changes in two firms' asset values
  • Equity return correlation - correlation of logarithmic changes in the market value of two firms' equity prices
  • Copula correlation - the values entered into the off-diagonal cells of the correlation matrix used in the copula approach
  • Spread correlation - correlation of changes in the spreads on two firms' comparable debt obligations
  • Implied credit correlation - estimate of the copula correlation derived from market prices

Structured Credit Motivations

  • Issuer:
    • Maturity matching
    • Aribtrage
    • Balance-sheet relief
  • Investor:
    • Access to diversified loan pools
    • Risk sharing via tranching
    • Embedded leverage
    • Yield chasing
    • Regulatory arbitrage
