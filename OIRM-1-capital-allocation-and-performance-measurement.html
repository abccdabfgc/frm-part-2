Capital Allocation and Performance Measurement

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Risk-Adjusted Return on Capital (RAROC)

  • Provides an economic basis to measure all relevant risk types and positions consistently
  • Generates appropriate risk/reward signals at all business levels
  • Affects how the bank allocates capital, performs risk analyses, adjusts pricing strategies, and many other processes

Capital

  • Economic capital
    • Cushion against various risks inherent in business
    • Absorbs unexpected losses, up to a certain confidence level
  • Regulatory capital
    • Derived from a set of rules to ensure that there is enough capital in the banking system

Risk Management Information System (MIS)

Definition: A system with sophisticated, detailed data collection, a powerful computation engine, massive storage capacity, and information distribution capability

RAROC 10 Commandments

  1. Include all business activities and global operations
  2. Implement a RAROC system impervious to arbitrage
  3. Be explicit, consistent, and congruent with other policies
  4. Recognize different types of capital, but emphasize economic capital
  5. Use a single risk-adjusted hurdle rate as the cost of capital
  6. Develop and implement an economic risk framework capturing credit, market, and operational risk
  7. Recognize funding and time-to-close liquidity
  8. Atribute capital as a function of risk
  9. Economic capital should be based on an appropriate confidence level
  10. Match revenues and risk charges
