Chapter 6

Liquidity and Leverage

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

  • Transactions liquidity - the ease of exchanging an asset for other assets
  • Funding liquidity - the ability to finance assets continuously at an acceptable rate

Funding Liquidity Risk

  • Maturity transformation - Providing long-term funding finaced with short-term funding (maturity mismatch)
  • Liquidity transformation - providing liquidity and payment services
  • Bank liquidity - make loans financed by deposits (fractional-reserve bank)

Structured Credit and Off-Balance-Sheet Funding

  • Provide matched funding for assets in a collateral pool
  • Two major types of off-balance-sheet vehicles:
    • Asset-backed commercial paper conduits
    • Structured investment vehicles (SIVs)

Markets for Collateral

  • Securities used as collateral are often lent yet again to another party, a process known as rehypothecation
  • Market structure
    • Haircut - ensures full value of collateral is not lent
    • Margin lending - lending used to finance a security transaction in which the loan is collateralized by the security
    • Repurchase agreements - matched pairs of the spot sale and forward repurchase of a security
    • Securities lending - lending a security to another party in exchange for a fee/rebate
    • Total return swaps - one party pays a fixed fee and receives the total return on a specified security

Leverage and Forms of Credit

  • Leverage ratio: L = A/E = (E+D)/E = 1+D/E
  • Leverage amplified sensitivity to changes in cash flow

Transactions Liquidity Risk

  • An asset is liquid if it resembles money and that its value is certain
  • Causes of transaction liquidity risk:
    • Cost of trade processing
    • Inventory management by dealers
    • Adverse selection
    • Differences of opinion
  • Characteristics of market liquidity:
    • Tightness - cost of a round-trip transaction
    • Depth - how large an order is required to move the market
    • Resiliency - length of time for which an order moves the market from equilibrium

Liquidity Risk Measurement

  • Measuring funding liquidity risk
    • Asset-liability management
  • Measuring transactions liquidity risk
    • Transaction cost liquidity risk
    • Adverse price impacts (LVaR)

Liquidity and System Risk

  • Fuding liquidity and solvency
    • Liquidity is the ability to meet immediate demand for cash
    • Solvency is when equity capital is positive or assets > liabilities
    • Illiquidity may lead to insolvency and vice-versa
  • Funding and market liquidity
    • Periods of illiquidity may lead to mark-to-market losses which can compromise a firm's solvency
  • Systemic risk
    • Disruptions in financial market infrastructure can lead to a chain of illuidity and/or insolvency
