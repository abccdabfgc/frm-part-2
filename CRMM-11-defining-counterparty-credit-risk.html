Chapter 11

Defining Counterparty Credit Risk

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

Definition: The risk that a counterparty in a derivatives transaction will default prior to expiration of a trade and fail to make the required current and future payments

  • The value of the underlying contract is uncertain both in magnitude and in direction

Components and Terminology

  • Credit exposure - loss in the event counterparty defaults
    • Actual and potential claims
  • Default probability and credit migration
  • Recovery rate - percentage of outstanding claims recovered
  • Mark-to-market - present value of all the payments to be received less those to be paid
  • Replacement cost

Controlling Counterparty Risk

  • Diversification - spreading exposure across different counterparties
  • Netting - offsetting positive and negative positions with the same counterparty
  • Collateralization - holding cash or securities against an exposure
  • Hedging - reducing exposure and counterparty risk via derivative instruments

Quantifying Counterparty Risk

  • Trade level - incorporates characteristics of the trade and associated risk factors
  • Counterparty level - incorporates risk mitigants for each counterparty
  • Portfolio level - considers the risk of all counterparties

Credit Exposure Metrics

  • Expected MtM - forward or expected value of a transaction at some point in the future
  • Expected exposure (EE)- positive MtM exposures
  • Potential future exposure (PFE)
  • Expected positive exposure (EPE) - the average expected exposure through time
  • Effective EPE - a non-decreasing EE
  • Maximum PFE - the peak PFE value over a time period
