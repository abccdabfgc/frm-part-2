Pricing Counterparty Risk

  • Bilateral nature of credit exposure makes counterparty risk difficult to quantify
  • Credit value adjustment (CVA) - measures counterparty risk via a combination of:
    • The current risk-free value of the transaction(s)
    • A term representing the counterparty risk of the positions is subtracted out
    • CVA (assuming no wrong-way risk) ≈ Loss given default × Sum of (Discount factors × Expected exposure × Default probability)
    • CVA spread = CVA(t,T)/CDSpremium(t,T) = XCDS × EPE
For CVA spread: CDSpremium(t,T) is the unit premium value of a CDS and XCDS is the CDS premium corresponding to the maturity date T.

Bilateral Counterparty Risk

Bilateral CVAs are calculated under the assumption that both parties may default
