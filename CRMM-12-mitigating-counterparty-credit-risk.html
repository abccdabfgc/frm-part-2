Chapter 12

Mitigating Counterparty Credit Risk

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

  • Default-remote entities - entities with a low probability of default
  • Termination events - opportunity to terminate a transaction between inception and maturity
  • Netting - offsetting transactions when a counterparty is in default
  • Close-out - termination of all contracts between an insolvent and solvent counterparty
  • Collateralization - posting cash or securities as a guarantee against exposure

Default-Remote Entities

  • High-quality counterparties
  • Special purpose vehicles
  • Central counterparties

Termination and Walkaway Features

  • Termination events - break clauses, ratings triggers, mergers, management changes, NAV declines, key person events
  • Walkaway clause - allows a surviving institution to walk away from net liabilities to a counterparty in default
A break clause gives the holder the right to terminate a contract.

Netting and Close-Out

  • Netting - the right to offset amounts due at termination of separate contracts between the same counterparties
  • Close-out - the right to terminate contracts unilaterally under certain conditions

Collateral

Collateralization Specifications

  • Linkage of collateral parameters to credit quality
  • Frequency of margin calls
  • Threshold - a level of exposure below which collateral will not be called
  • Initial margin - collateral that is posted upfront
  • Minimum transfer amount - smallest amount of collateral that can be transferred
  • Rounding - collateral calls will be round up or down to certain lot sizes
  • Haircuts - a discount applied to the value of collateral
  • Coupons and interest payments
  • Substitution and rehypothecation
