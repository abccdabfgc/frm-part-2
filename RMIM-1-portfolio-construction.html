Steps in Alpha Analysis

  1. Scale the alphas: α = Volatility × IC × Score
  2. Trim outliers
  3. Remove biases or undesirable bets (neutralization)
    • Benchmark-neutralization
    • Cash-neutralization
    • Risk-factor-neutralization
IC = information coefficient. Benchmark-neutralization set the benchmark alpha to 0. Cash-neutralization sets the alphas such that there is not active cash position. Risk-factor-neutralization sets the alphas of risk factors to 0.

Transaction Costs

Definition: The cost of moving from one portfolio to another

  • The annualized transactions cost is the round-trip cost divided by the holding period in years
  • Frequent rebalancing means high transaction costs so there is a tradeoff between expected active return, active risk, and transaction costs

Techniques for Portfolio Construction

  • Screenining
    1. Rank stocks by alpha
    2. Select first X stocks
    3. Weight the stocks
  • Stratification - a screening technique that ensures the sample is representative of the total population
  • Linear programming - characterizes stocks along risk dimensions
  • Quadratic programming - explicitly considers alpha, risk, and transaction costs

Dispersion

Definition: The difference between the maximum return and minimum return for separate account portfolios

  • Client-driven dispersion due to different constraints
  • Manager-driven dispersion: lack of attention, transaction costs, etc.
