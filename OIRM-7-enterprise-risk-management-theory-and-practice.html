Enterprise Risk Management: Theory and Practice

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

Definition: A risk management approach that views all risks together within a coordinated and strategic framework

ERM and Shareholder Value

  • Macro benefits
    • Limits probability that cash shortfalls will lead to value-destroying investment cuts
    • Reduces exposure to non-core risks
  • Micro benefits
    • Forces business managers to consider impact of all material risks in investment and operatin decisions

Determining Appropriate Risk Levels

  • Tradeoff between active risk management and size of cash and equity buffer
  • Target credit ratings may inform decision

ERM Conceptual Framework

  1. Management determines risk appetite
  2. Management estimates the amount of capital required to support target risk level
  3. Management determines optimal combination of capital and risk expected to yield target rating
  4. Management decentralizes risk-capital tradeoff using capital allocation and performance evaluation systems

Implementation Challenges

  1. Identification and quantification of risks
  2. Distinguishing between economic value and accounting performance
  3. Risk aggregation
  4. Risk measurement
  5. Distinguishing between regulatory and economic capital
