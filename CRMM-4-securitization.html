Chapter 4

Securitization

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Introduction

Definition: The process by which a firms sells credit-sensitive assets to a third party that in turn issues securities whose cash flows are backed by the original assets as collateral

Securitization Process

1. Cash flow on assets; 2. Asset ownership conveyed to SPE in true sale; 3. Purchase price for assets; 4. Fees; 5. Advisory/monitoring services; 6. Issuance of securities; 7. Initial purchase price for securities; 8. Assets deposited in trust or custodial account; 9. Cash flow on assets; 10. Custodial fees; 11. Initial purchase price for securities; 12. Distribution of securities; 13. Principal and interest on securities;

Major Players

  • Sponsor - institution that initiates the securitization
  • Originator - original asset owner
  • Transferee - counterparty to the bilateral asset sale
  • Trustee - investors' agent
  • Custodian/Servicer - collects and disburses cash flows
  • Structuring agent - originator's advisor
  • Underwriter - markets and distributes the securities issued by transferee
  • Rating agencies - render assessments of credit quality
  • Law firms - provide legal counsel
  • Regulatory agencies
  • External risk transfer and risk finance counterparties

Consolidation

In order to avoid consolidating the assets back on the originator's balance sheet, a 'true sale' must occur'

Credit Enhancement

  • Internal:
    • Direct equity issue
    • Holdback
    • Cash collateral account
    • Excess spread
  • External:
    • Insurance, wraps, and guaranties
    • Letter of credit
    • Credit default swap
    • Put option on assets
Internal credit enhancements are provided by a participant inside the structure. External credit enhancements represent credit risk transfer from the SPE to anohter firm.

Liquidity Support

  • Liquidity risk - risk that underlying assets serving as collateral may not generate enough cash to satisfy P&I obligations
  • Internal support:
    • Liability design and maturity structure
    • Reserves
  • External support:
    • Letters of credit with recourse
