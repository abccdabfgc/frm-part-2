Quantifying Counterparty Credit Exposure, 2 - The Impact of Collateral

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Remargin Period

Definition: Time period one should assume in a worst case scenario before taking delivery of collateral after a call

  • Valuation/margin call - time taken to compute current exposure and market value of collateral
  • Receiving collateral - delay due to processing a collateral request
  • Settlement - collateral may not be received immediately depending on settlement period
  • Grace period - period of time before a non-delivering party is deemed to be in default
  • Liquidation/close-out and re-hedge - time taken to liquidate collateral and close out and re-hedge positions

Modelling Collateral

  • Two sources of risk for a collateralized exposure:
    • Imperfect collateralization at a given date due to terms in the collateral agreement which do not permit a call for the full scredit support amount
    • Risk that exposure increases in-between margin calls making it impossible to collateralize that portion of the exposure
  • Parameters:
    • Remargin period
    • Threshold
    • Minimum transfer amount
    • Initial margin
    • Rounding

Collateralization Risks

  • Operational risk - missed collateral calls, failed deliveries, computer error, human error, fraud
  • Default risk - default relating to a security posted as collateral
  • FX risk
  • Liquidity and liquidation risk
