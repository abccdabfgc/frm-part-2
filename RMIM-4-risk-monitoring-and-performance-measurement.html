Risk Management as a Three-Legged Stool

  1. Planning
  2. Budgeting
  3. Monitoring

The Risk Plan

  1. Sets expected return and volatility goals for the relevant time period
  2. Defines points of success or failure
  3. Describes how risk capital will be deployed to achieve objectives
  4. Defines strategy in response to franchise-threatening events
  5. Identifies critical dependencies that exist inside and outside the organization

The Risk Budget

  • Quantifies the risk plan
  • For each risk allocation, there should be a corresponding return expectation

Risk Monitoring

Ensures that material deviations form the risk budget are detected and addressed in a timely manner

Risk Management Unit (RMU) Objectives

  • Gather, monitor, analyze, and distribute risk data
  • Develop a disciplined framework to address risk topics
  • Set and implement risk agenda and related initiatives
  • Monitor and identify risk trends
  • Promote discussion of risk issues throughout the organization
  • Promote risk awareness and a common vernacular
  • Ensure transaction are authorized in accordance with guidelines
  • Develop risk measurement and performance attribution tools
  • Develop an inventory of risk data
  • Establish risk and performance attribution systems

Perfomance Measurement Tools

  • The Green Zone - identifies instances of performance or achieved tracking error outside of normal expectations
  • Returns attribution - attributes the source of returns to individual securities and/or common factors
  • Sharpe ratio - a portfolio's excess return over the risk-free rate divided by its standard deviation
  • Information ratio - a portfolio's excess return over a benchmark divided by its tracking error
  • Alpha versus the benchmark - regresses the excess returns of a fund against the excess returns of its benchmark
  • Alpha versus peer group - regresses manager's excess returns against the excess returns of her peer group
