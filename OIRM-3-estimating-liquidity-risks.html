Estimating Liquidity Risks

Created for Alchemists Academy by MacLane Wilkison

Liquidity and Liquidity Risks

  • Liquidity refers to a trader's ability to execute a trade or liquidate a position
  • Depends on the number of traders in the market, frequency and size of trades, time to carry out a trade, and cost of transacting
  • Bid-ask spread and market price
The bid-ask spread denotes the price a security may be sold (bid) or purchased (ask). The market price is a fictional price that is the average of the bid and ask.

Estimating Liquidity-Adjusted VaR

  • Constant spread approach
    • Liquidity cost (LC) = 0.5 × spread × P
    • LVaR = VaR + LC
  • Exogenous spread approach
    • Assumes that traders face random spreads and uses Monte Carlo simulation to estimate the LVaR
  • Endogenous-price approaches
    • Accounts for market's response to a trade
    • Can easily be added to an exogenous spread approach
  • Liquidity discount approach
Constant spread approach: Spread = actual spread divided by the midpoint and P = the size of position to be liquidated. The liquidity discount approach suggests three modifications to traditional VaR: (1) use an optimal holding period rather than an arbitrary one; (2) Add the average liquidity discount to the trader's losses to account for the expected loss of the selling process; (3) account for the volatility of the time to liquidation and of the liquidity discount factor.

Estimating Liquidity at Risk (LaR)

  • LaR refers to the risk attached to prospective cash flows over a defined horizon period
  • Maximum likely cash outflow over the period at a given confidence level
  • Common types of cash flows to consider:
    • Known certain cash flows
    • Unknowns uncertain cash flows
    • Conditional uncertain cash flows
